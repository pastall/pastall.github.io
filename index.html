Our Story

How did we get here
Well, we’ve known each other now for the best part of 9 years and together for 6 of them...

Jan and I originally met in a night club in Manchester’s Northern Quarter called North (fond memories of that club!), where I got introduced to her by a friend. I was pretty clear that my name was indeed Phil (not surprising) when I introduced myself, yet Jan insisted to call me Paul for the duration of the night. Oh, and to top things off, Jan was in her renowned ‘torment mode’ and I ended up with a bucket of ice all over me as she was chasing someone around the room having a water fight… Needless to say, Jan made a great impression on me that evening!

Our paths would always cross (as we were/are part of the same circle of friends), from then on in, and Jan eventually got to grips calling me Phil! Phew! Sure enough, our friendship would grow and we became more than friends and now we're at this very special stage in our lives!

The Plot

Getting the ring on Jan’s finger
I had plotted for nearly 6 months plus to achieve the most amazing location, ring and environment to ask that big question. The chosen venue was Reykjavik, Iceland whilst we attempted to achieve a life long ambition to see the Northern Lights.

Friday: The first evening was a flop, totally clouded over so no chance of seeing them.

Saturday: Eating our evening meal we decided no matter what that we’d be stood outside in an attempt to find them. You are told to take pictures even if you can’t see them in the skies as a camera can show them much sooner than the eyes can pick them up. I became 'the happy snapper' and as luck would have it, one of the pictures showed the start of a light display! We spent the next hour watching the show peak and slow down and at that point we decided to head back in, it was freezing! At that point, I asked Jan to come outside on our balcony overlooking the fjord, which she did (reluctantly) – It was here that I asked her to marry me. As luck would have it, the lights were still showing as well so someone really was looking over me that night!

Sunday (Our Last Evening): We saw THE MOST AMAZING light show (a picture of which you can see above!)...

Not too bad a result, 2 nights from 3 seeing the Northern Lights and getting engaged as well! PERFECT...

The location is Dubrovnik, Croatia. Dubrovnik being 'The Pearl of The Adriatic'. Fans know it as King's Landing, but when you strip it down there's a lot more to Dubrovnik than its starring role in Game of Thrones!

Some Things To Know
Dress Code - "Beach Formal"
Think elegant wedding - so dress to impress, but also dress for the elements (sun and water). Anything you'd wear to a nice restaurant on a summers day is appropriate.

He should wear: A summer suit with a linen shirt (no ties required), linen pants etc.
She should wear: A formal summer outfit at tea/knee or long length etc.

Children

Unfortunately due to the location (reception is right next to the water with no fencing), timing and late night bar, we will not be able to extend the wedding invitations to children. If you could be so kind to respect our wishes for an “adult only wedding“ it would be much appreciated.
